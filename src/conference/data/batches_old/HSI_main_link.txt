Call for Papers 
 We are pleased to announce that we await your contribution for the 2025 edition of the HSI conference! We warmly invite researchers, practitioners, and thought leaders to share their original insights that delve into groundbreaking research, innovative applications, and essential challenges in human-system interaction. This conference offers a dynamic platform for resounding exchanges of ideas, knowledge, and creativity in this exciting field. With past editions in inspiring locations like Paris, Gdańsk, Melbourne, and Tokyo, we’ve seen a fantastic global interest in these topics.Join us in Ulsan, South Korea, from July 16 to 19, 2025, for the 17th International Conference on Human System Interaction.Thanks to our partners in IEEE IES, past proceedings have found a home in IEEE Xplore. We can’t wait to see your contributions and foster meaningful discussions together! 
 Technical tracks: 
 1. Deep Learning and Computer Vision 
 2. Human System Interaction in Biomedical Engineering 
 3. Advances in AI and Learning in Healthcare 
 4. Applications 
 5. Generative AI & NLP 
 6. Theory and Technologies on Human Factors in Advanced Human-Machine Environment 
 7. Face & Audio Analysis 
 8. Machine Learning Applications 
 9. Neurodegenerative Diseases & Cognitive Assessment 
 Submission process 
 Prospective authors are invited to electronically submit full regular papers of their work using the IES submission system (href="https://confcomm.ieee-ies.org/app/general/conferences/HSI25/initial-submission" - https://confcomm.ieee-ies.org/app/general/conferences/HSI25/initial-submission
). The authors will need to register in order to use the system. After registering, wait for the confirmation email and verify your email address. If you don’t get the confirmation email or have some problem with your submission, please contact ieee.ies.hsi@gmail.com. 
 Format&Length 
 Regular papers are limited to six (6) pages of A4 or letter format. An additional 2 pages can be included at an extra fee. 
 IEEE is strict about the requirements for PDF files for inclusion in the IEEE Xplore®️ Digital Library. It is strongly recommended to use the set of templates in MS Word and LaTeX formats provided by IEEE. (Find templates inhttps://www.ieee.org/conferences/publishing/templates.html) 
 To learn more about the formatting of papers for IEEE IES conferences, please visithttps://www.ieee-ies.org/conferences/paper-format-for-conferences. 
 Originality 
 Please make sure that only original work is being submitted (not previously published/copyrighted). The conference will be using the CrossCheck automated screening system to verify the originality of papers. IEEE takes disciplinary actions when violations are detected. 
 Final Submission & Copyright 
 You need to submit the final version of your paper in IES submission system, with the modifications requested by the reviewers. Once you have done so, you will have a new option in the submission system labelled “sign IEEE copyright,” which will take you to IEEE eCF site, where you will be able to transfer copyright to IEEE. Do not add any copyright notice in the bottom margin of your first page. The correct copyright notice will be added by the post-production team. 
 Publication 
 All regular papers presented during the conference will be submitted to IEEE Xplore Digital Library.
