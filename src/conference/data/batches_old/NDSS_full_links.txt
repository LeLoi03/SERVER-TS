 NDSS Symposium 2025 
 The Network and Distributed System Security (NDSS) Symposium 2025 was held from 24 to 28 February 2025 in San Diego, California. 
 Almost 700 leading academics, industry researchers, and security practitioners from around the world gathered in San Diego, California, for the 32nd edition of the Network and Distributed System Security (NDSS) Symposium to discuss the latest research and developments in Internet security research. 
 The five-day event started with keynote presentations fromhref="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/keynote-by-dr-johanna-sepulveda/" - Dr Johanna Sepúlveda
(Airbus Defence and Space) on the different quantum technologies and their potential transformative capabilities.href="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/keynote-by-dr-kathleen-fisher/" - Dr Kathleen Fisher
(DARPA) reminded us that an increasingly hostile world needs increasingly secure, distributed, and resilient systems. 
 The week continued withhref="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/accepted-papers/" - 211 paper presentations
in four parallel tracks andhref="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/accepted-posters/" - 37 poster presentations
. Alongside the main symposium,href="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/co-located-events/" - eight co-located events were held
, and almost 100 additional papers were presented during these events.

 August 13–15, 2025 
 Seattle, WA, USA 
 The34th USENIX Security Symposiumwill take place on August 13–15, 2025, at the Seattle Convention Center in Seattle, WA, USA. The USENIX Security Symposium brings together researchers, practitioners, system programmers, and others interested in the latest advances in the security and privacy of computer systems and networks.

 NDSS Symposium 2025 
 The Network and Distributed System Security (NDSS) Symposium 2025 was held from 24 to 28 February 2025 in San Diego, California. 
 Almost 700 leading academics, industry researchers, and security practitioners from around the world gathered in San Diego, California, for the 32nd edition of the Network and Distributed System Security (NDSS) Symposium to discuss the latest research and developments in Internet security research. 
 The five-day event started with keynote presentations fromhref="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/keynote-by-dr-johanna-sepulveda/" - Dr Johanna Sepúlveda
(Airbus Defence and Space) on the different quantum technologies and their potential transformative capabilities.href="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/keynote-by-dr-kathleen-fisher/" - Dr Kathleen Fisher
(DARPA) reminded us that an increasingly hostile world needs increasingly secure, distributed, and resilient systems. 
 The week continued withhref="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/accepted-papers/" - 211 paper presentations
in four parallel tracks andhref="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/accepted-posters/" - 37 poster presentations
. Alongside the main symposium,href="https://www.ndss-symposium.org/ndss2025/co-located-events/" - eight co-located events were held
, and almost 100 additional papers were presented during these events.
