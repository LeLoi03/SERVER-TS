 The International Society for Presence Research (ISPR)is a non-profit membership organization founded in 2002 to support academic research related to the concept of (tele)presence. 
 Telepresence, often shortened to presence, is commonly referred to as a sense of ‘being there’ in a virtual environment and more broadly defined as an illusion of nonmediation in which users of any technology overlook or misconstrue the technology’s role in their experience (seehref="https://ispr.info/about-presence-2/about-presence/" - Presence defined
for more details). 
 This web site is intended to serve as a resource for those who conduct research, develop theory, design, market, write about, or simply are interested in, the concept and phenomena of presence. 
 We encourage your questions, contributions, comments and suggestions regarding ISPR and all aspects of this website; please feel free to contact us viahref="https://ispr.info/home/contact/" - our webform
(or send an e-mail tolombard@temple.edu).
