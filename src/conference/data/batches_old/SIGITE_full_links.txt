SIGITE is the Association for Computing Machinery’s 
 Special Interest Group on Information Technology Education. 
 Our members include information technology faculty (teachers and researchers), students, and industry professionals. 
 Mission 
 SIGITE’s mission is to provide a forum for the interaction of practitioners, educators, and others in the field of Information Technology (IT) to exchange ideas and engage in activities that advance the knowledge of its members. 
 With over 400 members worldwide, SIGITE is driving the articulation, promotion, and dissemination of the computing discipline of Information Technology. The organization has created a model undergraduate curriculum and helped create accreditation guidelines for IT programs, and is now defining and promoting IT research.

SIGITE 2024 – 25nd Annual Conference on IT Education – Call for Papers 
 March 4, 2024 
 Categories:href="https://www.sigite.org/?cat=1" - Uncategorized
by admin 
 El Paso Community College will be the host of the 25th ACM Annual Conference on Information Technology Education (SIGITE 2024), which will take place at theEl Paso Community College—Valle Verde CampusfromOctober 10 to 12, 2024. This conference is dedicated to recognizing the dynamic interplay between emerging technologies and the incorporation of artificial intelligence-based tools in educational strategies and pedagogy. 
 You can submit your proposals for papers, panels, workshops, posters, and lightning talks that address this theme or other topics related to IT education until June 1st, 2024. In addition, SIGITE 2024 also accepts student papers. The conference encourages contributions from students as well as IT educators, researchers, and practitioners. The Call for Papers (CFP) will be posted here soon. 
 Learn More About Submissions and Submitting 
 To know everything you need to know about submitting to the SIGITE 2024 conference, please visit the submission page on the conference website.

EducationCybersecurityEL PASO, Texas (KFOX14/CBS4) —Artificial intelligence in academia is taking center stage during a weekend-long conference hosted by El Paso Community College. 
 The25th Annual Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) Conference on Special Interest Group on Information Technology Education (SIGITE) kicked off at EPCC's Valle Verde campus on Thursday. 
 "[The conference] is important for El Paso because it gave us a showcase where to bring different educators from different places of the world, showcasing what is happening here in the city and the region, but also to show the talent that is around El Paso," said Christian Servin, a professor of computing at EPCC.The 25th Annual Association for Computing Machinery Conference on Special Interest Group on Information Technology Education kicked off at EPCC's Valle Verde campus on Thursday. October 10, 2024. (credit: KFOX14/CBS4) 
 Over the course of three days, cybersecurity professionals, national and international speakers, and education professionals will participate in lightning talks, panels and workshops. 
 According to the organizers, the ACM SIGITE conference is "dedicated to recognizing the dynamic interplay between emerging technologies and the incorporation of artificial intelligence-based tools in educational strategies and pedagogy." 
 "Those are the highlights," Servin said of the conference. "The emerging computing areas that is being used at every level from your cell phone, your mobile device, all the way to the things that you put in the cloud. It is important to recognize the best ways to teach these categories of AI for educators so we can actually disseminate this to our students."
